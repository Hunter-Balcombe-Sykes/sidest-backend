## Plain English

When a visitor submits a contact form enquiry, the system sends an email notification to the professional. The email's subject line was built by combining the visitor's name and their message subject directly — both can be up to 100 characters each, so the subject could end up well over 200 characters long. That's outside standard email limits and can cause display issues or spam scoring problems. The fix caps the subject line at 77 characters (the RFC-recommended soft limit), appending "..." if it would have been longer.

## Technical Summary

**Files changed:** `app/Mail/SiteEnquiryNotification.php`

**Before:** `subject: "New enquiry from {$this->enquiry->name} — {$this->enquiry->subject}"` — raw user input, up to ~222 chars.

**After:** `subject: Str::limit("New enquiry from {$this->enquiry->name} — {$this->enquiry->subject}", 77)` — capped at 77 chars with trailing `...` if truncated.

**Contract:** The DB allows `name` and `subject` each up to 100 chars (enforced by `PublicEnquiryRequest` validation and the `site.enquiries` schema). The truncation happens at the email layer only; stored data is unchanged.
