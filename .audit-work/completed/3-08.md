# #3-08 — Plan upgrade local state may diverge from Stripe past_due

## Plain English

When someone upgrades their paid plan and the payment fails, Stripe marks the subscription as "past due" but still tells us what plan they tried to upgrade to. Our webhook handler was treating that as a successful upgrade and unlocking the higher-tier features right away — even though no money had changed hands. Now we check whether Stripe is happy with the payment before promoting the plan locally. If payment later succeeds, Stripe fires the same webhook again with a healthy status, and we promote the plan at that point.

## Technical Summary

**File changed:** `app/Http/Controllers/Api/Webhooks/StripeWebhookController.php`

In `handleSubscriptionUpdated()` (line ~172), the block that resolves a new `Plan` from the Stripe price ID and writes it to `plan_id` is now guarded by `in_array($subscription->status, ['active', 'trialing'], true)`. The `status` column is still updated unconditionally (correctly reflecting `past_due`, etc.). On a successful payment retry, Stripe fires another `customer.subscription.updated` with `status=active`, at which point the `plan_id` is promoted.

`ChangeProfessionalPlanAction` required no changes — it already defers local plan reconciliation to the webhook.

**Tests added:** `tests/Feature/Stripe/StripeWebhookSubscriptionUpdatedTest.php`
- `it does not promote plan_id when status is past_due` — asserts status updates but plan_id stays at old plan
- `it promotes plan_id when retry succeeds and status becomes active` — asserts the recovery path works

## Decisions Made

- **Guard on Stripe raw status (`$subscription->status`), not the mapped local status**: Makes the intent explicit — we're checking Stripe's payment state, not our internal enum. Also means `trialing` (which maps to `active` locally) naturally passes the guard.
- **No change to `ChangeProfessionalPlanAction`**: The action's docblock already documents async reconciliation via webhook; the root cause was entirely in the handler.
- **`invoice.paid` handler left unchanged**: It resets status to active on successful retry but doesn't set `plan_id` — that's correct because a companion `subscription.updated` with `status=active` will fire and handle the promotion.

## Notes

Downgrade (paid→lower paid) is unaffected: prorations on a price decrease are credits, so the subscription stays `active` and plan_id is correctly synced. The guard only prevents premature promotion on upgrades where the proration charge can fail.
