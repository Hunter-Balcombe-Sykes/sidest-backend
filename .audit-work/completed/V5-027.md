# #V5-027 — Enquiry notification email lacks per-brand throttle — spammable inbox

## Plain English

When someone submits a contact form on a brand's public site, Side St sends a notification email to the brand's inbox. The problem was that bots rotating IP addresses could flood a brand with hundreds of notification emails per minute by bypassing the existing per-IP throttle. The fix adds a per-brand limit of 10 notification emails per hour: the enquiry is always saved (no lead data is lost), but any notification emails beyond the limit are silently suppressed. The limit is configurable via an environment variable.

## Technical Summary

**Files changed:**
- `app/Http/Controllers/Api/PublicSite/PublicEnquiryController.php` — Imported `RateLimiter` facade. In `submit()`, the `SendEnquiryNotificationJob` dispatch is now guarded by `RateLimiter::tooManyAttempts('enquiry_notify:{professional_id}', $limit)`. On each allowed dispatch, `RateLimiter::hit($key, 3600)` increments the per-brand counter with a 1-hour sliding window TTL.
- `config/sidest.php` — Added `throttle.enquiry_notification_per_hour` (default 10, overridable via `SIDEST_ENQUIRY_NOTIFY_PER_HOUR`).
- `tests/Feature/Contact/PublicEnquirySubmissionTest.php` — Added test `stops dispatching notification job once per-brand hourly limit is exceeded but still returns 200`: sets limit to 2, submits 3 enquiries from distinct emails, asserts exactly 2 `SendEnquiryNotificationJob` dispatches and 3 saved enquiry rows.

**Throttle key:** `enquiry_notify:{professional_id}` — scoped to the brand, not the IP or submitter email, so rotating IPs cannot evade it.

## Decisions Made

- **Throttle in controller not in job:** The controller is the right layer — it prevents unnecessary job dispatches and keeps the rate-limiting logic co-located with the other abuse guards (honeypot, timing check).
- **RateLimiter over raw Cache::increment:** `RateLimiter` manages the companion `{key}:timer` key automatically, giving a correct sliding window without manual TTL bookkeeping.
- **Scope by professional_id not notification_email:** `professional_id` is the meaningful tenant boundary; email could change or be shared across brands. Scoping to the brand's identity is more robust.
- **Suppress silently, return 200:** Enquiry is always persisted. The caller (the submitter) has no reason to know a notification was throttled; returning an error would confuse legitimate users and expose the limit to attackers.
- **Default of 10/hour:** Generous enough for real traffic spikes (flash sales, press coverage), strict enough to cap bot abuse at a manageable level. Env-var override allows adjustment per environment without a deploy.

## Notes

The existing `throttle:leads` middleware (3/min/IP, 100/min/subdomain) guards the HTTP layer; this new RateLimiter guard is a second, independent defense at the business logic layer. The two throttles are complementary: the HTTP throttle stops per-IP floods; the brand-level throttle caps the total inbox impact regardless of how many IPs are used.

`Cache::flush()` in the test `beforeEach` resets the `RateLimiter` backing store (Redis-backed cache), so existing tests are unaffected.
